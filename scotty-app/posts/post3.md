title: Tuesday, Raining
date: 2022-05-22

---

The cafe was almost empty. That was the whole point.

She came every Tuesday when it rained, which in this city happened often enough to be reliable. She ordered the same thing, black coffee, a croissant she would mostly not eat, and sat at the window table, the one with the wobbly leg that nobody else wanted.

Outside, people moved past with their umbrellas tilted against the wind. She liked watching them. Everyone in the rain looked briefly like they were thinking about something important.

A man stopped just outside the window to check his phone. His umbrella dripped. A pigeon regarded him seriously from the awning above.

She thought about a story she hadn't written yet, about a woman who collects the minutes other people lose, the minute you spend staring at the wall, the minute between one thought and the next. What would she do with them all? Build something, maybe. A house made of in-between moments.

The coffee cooled. The rain picked up.

The pigeon was still watching the man with the phone, patient and ancient and completely unimpressed.

She opened her notebook. Tuesdays were good for starting things.
